Programming Projects

Automated Hollowing for 3D Scanned Models (Internship)

During the summer of 2015, I worked at a startup called Twindom.

I built a system of scripts using various 3D packages that would take the model generated from the scan data, perform various repairs to the mesh, and hollow it by creating an encapsulated inner shell, cutting printing costs by ~90%.

In the image to the right, the inner shell is highlighted. The volume of the inner shell is the amount of printing material saved.
